Lab: Modeling a Dependent SI/SIS Epidemic

Data Description

The HPTN-036 study interviewed men who have sex with men (MSM) in six Peruvian cities, as part of a study of acceptability for an intervention of HSV-2 suppression as a method to reduce HIV transmission. In this study, men were asked partner-specific qustions for their most recent sex partners in the last year (up to 3). Among the questions they were asked include whether they anticipated having sex with the partners again (options = yes, no, donâ€™t know). For those relationships in which men expected to have sex with the partner again, the mean age of the relationship at the time of interview was 26 months. If one also includes relationships in which the respondent said they were unsure of having sex again, the mean relational age was 22 months.

Tasks (Continued from Day 2)

  1. Build a dynamic network animation by simplifying your Day 2 model down to a small size (n = 100), recalculating the network statistics, running a single simulation over a short number of time steps, and following the animation tutorial code.

Then:

  1. Incorporate vital dynamics for your model by adjusting the model parameterization for birth and death rates in both the network model estimation and simulation. Simulate this over an SI or SIS disease type. For this, make sure to conduct post-simulation diagnostics and compare against the pre-simulation diagnostics.
  2. Consider new model parameterizations for the network, including serosorting or forms of differential activity or mixing by attributes.
  3. Simulate counterfactual epidemic models varying key biological or clinical parameters, and calculate some summary statistics comparing the two scenarios by extracting the data frame (e.g., number or proportion of infections averted in scenario 1 vs scenario 2).
