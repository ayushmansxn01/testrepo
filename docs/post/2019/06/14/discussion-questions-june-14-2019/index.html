Discussion Questions: June 14, 2019

Eva Enns

2019/06/14

We envision different kinds of analyses that could be done with the WHAMP model related to ADAP and/or PrEP-DAP. We would like your thoughts on the kinds of analyses that would be of greatest interest to you and the kinds of outputs / projections that would be most useful in informing your work.

Evaluating impact of program changes

1. What are the kinds of ADAP and/or PrEP-DAP changes/scenarios that you would like to evaluate using the WHAMP model?

Examples of external changes (not under DOH control):

  • Elimination of drug subsidies

  • Changes in the cost of drugs, care, or insurance premium costs

Examples of programmatic changes:

  • Changing program eligibility, such as increasing or decreasing income elgibility thresholds for ADAP

  • Imposing an income threshold on PrEP-DAP

2. For external changes in particular, how would the program respond to budget reductions?

Your input is needed to appropriately implement these scenarios in the model (and capture how reduced spending would impact HIV outcomes).

Examples:

  • Prioritize existing clients and limit new enrollment to stay within budget

  • Prioritize enrollment by income level (would current clients ever be disenrolled?)

3. For each scenario, we will project expected epidemiological outcomes (incidence, prevalence, total infections over a specified time horizon). Are there other outcomes of interest?

Examples:

  • ADAP and/or PrEP-DAP program size over time

  • ADAP and/or PrEP-DAP expenditures over time

  • ART and/or PrEP coverage

Projections of the current programs

1. The model can also be used to project outcomes into the future (under current programs/policies). Are there specific outcomes that would be of interest to DOH?

Examples:

  • HIV prevalence and incidence over time, with a particular focus on “end the epidemic” targets

  • ADAP and/or PrEP-DAP program size or expenditures over time

  • Estimation of infections averted by ADAP and/or PrEP-DAP over a defined time horizon (we would model the counterfactual where these programs do not exist at all)
